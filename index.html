Drug Crisis in Phoenix, Az.

Rise of deaths due to opioids.

In Arizona, there were 2,006 opioid confirmed fatalities last year – an 80% rise compared with 2018. Of the deaths in 2021, 94% involved synthetics like fentanyl, up from 69% in 2018. While heroin deaths dropped by 68% over the same period.

01

Fentanyl

According to media reports, drug traffickers are using a new strategy known as "rainbow fentanyl" to market extremely addictive and potentially lethal fentanyl to children and teenagers in the form of candy. A synthetic opioid called fentanyl is 50 times more potent than heroin and 100 times more strong than morphine.

Crystal Meth

Resembles broken pieces of glass, is a prescription medicine that has been unlawfully altered and combined with over-the-counter drugs in meth labs. Really addictive that causes elevated blood pressure, heart rate, elevated breathing rate, body temperature and anxiety. High doses may result in cardiac arrest, stroke, convulsions, or even death.

Heroin

An opioid drug made from morphine, a natural substance taken from the seed pod of various opium poppy plants. Heroin can be a white or brown powder, or a black sticky substance known as black tar heroin. People inject, sniff, snort, or smoke heroin.

Help is available..

If you or someone you know is struggling with addiction, please urge them to work with their medical service provider, reach out through the Angel Initiative, or the Governor’s Office of Youth, Faith, and Family.

We urge those at risk individuals to seek assistance from any reputable treatment facility before another life is lost.

Unwanted or expired medications may be disposed of in the Med Return Boxes located in the lobbies of CVS Pharmacys and most Phoenix Police Stations.
